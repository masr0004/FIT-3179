Crops Yield Per State

Vegetables and cash crops dominate as the highest-yielding categories across Malaysian states, with vegetables leading in Kelantan (28.9 t/ha) and cash crops performing strongly in Perak (17.1 t/ha) and Selangor (14.3 t/ha). Overall, Paddy consistently shows the lowest yields across states, while the median yields reveal a more balanced distribution with crops like spices, industrial crops, and coconut representing mid-range performance.

Fertilizer Use vs Agricultural GDP

Indexed trends (0-1 scale) comparing fertilizer use and agricultural GDP, showing diverging patterns (2010-2021)

Fertilizer Consumption vs Agriculture GDP%

From 2012 onwards, Malaysia's fertilizer consumption remained high while agricultural GDP contribution declined consistently, suggesting increased fertilizer use alone does not guarantee GDP growth. Other factors like market demand, land constraints, or diversification matter more for economic contribution. The 2020 COVID-19 shock affected both metrics, but fertilizer use recovered faster than GDP, implying supply chain disruptions impacted the sector more severely.

Agricultural Production and GDP Trends: Malaysia (2010-2021)

Agricultural production showed varied patterns with crop production peaking in 2015 and 2017 (lows in 2010 and 2016) and food production highest in 2016 and 2020 (lows in 2010 and 2011), yet the sector's GDP contribution declined consistently throughout 2010-2019, with a notable recovery in 2021 where both production indices and GDP showed upward trends. Production remained stable despite economic pressures, suggesting the 2020 COVID-19 period may have intensified existing challenges affecting agriculture's economic value rather than its output capacity.

Food Inflation Across the Years

Monthly food price inflation trends across years (2010-2021). Note: Tooltips were omitted as all inflation values are explicitly labeled on the chart, making hover interactions redundant for this visualization format.

Food Price Inflation ‚Äî Monthly Trends by Year (2010-2021, Malaysia)

Food inflation in Malaysia showed considerable volatility across the decade, with peak rates occurring in 2011 (October at 5.7%), 2016 (March at 5.0%), and 2019 (August at 2.6%), while experiencing dramatic lows during 2018 (reaching near-zero in mid-year) and 2020 (dropping to 0.8% in February). The 2010-2013 period displayed consistently higher inflation rates (2-5%), while post-2017 years exhibited more moderate and stable patterns (mostly 1-4%), suggesting Malaysia's food prices became more controlled in recent years despite occasional spikes, with the COVID-19 period (2020) showing surprisingly low inflation before gradually recovering in 2021.

Imports vs Exports

Malaysia's Agricultural Trade Transition (2010-2021)

Malaysia's agricultural exports declined steadily from 3.17% to 1.43% over the decade, while imports remained relatively stable around 2.0-2.5%. By 2021, imports (2.50%) surpassed exports (1.43%) for the first time, indicating Malaysia shifted from a net agricultural exporter to a net importer. This pattern might suggest that Malaysia's agricultural sector is becoming increasingly reliant on foreign food sources rather than domestic production for export.

Agricultural Employment by Gender: Malaysia (2010-2021)

Agricultural employment in Malaysia declined from 16% in 2010 to 12.8% in 2021, with males consistently maintaining nearly double the participation rate of females throughout the period. This pattern might suggest that Malaysia's agricultural sector is losing workforce participation across the board, with men continuing to dominate agricultural labor despite overall sector decline, potentially reflecting traditional gender roles or the physically demanding nature of agricultural work.
